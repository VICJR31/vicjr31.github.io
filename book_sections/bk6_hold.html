American Empire: The Center Cannot Hold (2002)

The decade following the end of the Great War brought a dramatic change in power. And those aiming to regain their former glory of the past. Yet, when the Stock Market crashes, due to Russia being unable to repay Austro-Hungarian banks from the war, it sends the world into a profound depression. As a dying radical right-wing party, the Freedom Party gains moment at an astonishing scale. With their leader, Jake Featherston, steering to do more than bring the Confederate States off its knees.

The Pacific War

The Great Depression, caused by the economic 'bubble' built over the 1920s until finally bursting, throws many world leaders and their countries down an uncertain, downward spiral. Scrambling to try and maintain the panic. U.S. President Blackford, elected in 1928 (a year before the crash), is blamed heavily. As he tries and fails to set-up programs that prove costly and ineffective. With makeshift homes built by the homeless sardonically calling them "Blackfordburghs." Costing Blackford his bid for reelection in 1933 to Calvin Coolidge (later Herbert Hoover after Coolidge, allegedly, killed himself in his bathtub). Putting a final nail to his coffin when the Empire of Japan launched an undetected air raid on Los Angles during a small rally Blackford held.

Despite the war starting in 1932 between the United States and Japan, the former spotted a Japanese supply ship sending arms to Canadian rebels. Japan nearly getting in the first lick when their submarine tried and failed to sink the USS Remembrance . Sparking a war between the two growing powers. Dragging on until the start of Hoover's term around 1934. While both sides agree to a return to the status quo, for now. And the U.S. able to somewhat begin rearming its forces.

Radical Changes

However, with the Confederates States hit harder more than ever from this panic. Groups like the Freedom Party had already begun flexing its muscles in secret by staying afloat. And sending volunteers down in Mexico from its Civil War back in the 20s (with some testing out the new armored barrels to help monarchist forces prevail). With more and more Confederates turning to extremism as C.S. President Burton Mitchel, like his U.S. counterparts, falls flat to provide adequate aid.

A Shadow Brewing

Soon the once-dominant party in the Confederate States, the Whig party, collapses alongside the Radical Liberals. Many of their followers, and other militant groups such as the Tin Hats and the Redemption League, absorbed by the Freedom Party. Leaving the streets and cities, more promptly in Richmond, to be light up with thousands of torches. And the thousands and thousands of Confederate battle flags' reverse colors waving proudly. With massive crowds yelling "Freedom!" as they prowled on their marches. Many intimidating, attacking, and, sometimes, killing black Confederates and those that distaste the party with unrelenting discrimination. But this only becomes the beginning.

Finally, following the election of 1933, Jake Featherston, leader of the Freedom Party, after a failed grab for power back in the 20s and an even bleaker one for the six years after the Calkins incident. Stands on the Gray House's podium, the equivalent to the US White House, to be inaugurated as the 13th President of the Confederate States of America. Intending to place those who wrong him and his country, Over Open Sights .
